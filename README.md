# AI Study Planner

Building AI course project

## Summary

My project idea is an AI-based study planner for students. The purpose of the tool is to help students organize their study time in a more realistic and less stressful way. By looking at deadlines, exam dates, and the amount of free time a student has, the system could suggest a study plan that is easier to follow and better balanced.

## Background

A lot of students struggle with planning their time. This becomes a bigger problem when several courses have deadlines around the same period, or when exams are close together. Many students know what they need to do, but they do not always know how to divide the work into smaller steps or when to start. Because of this, many people end up procrastinating, feeling overwhelmed, or doing everything at the last minute.

This idea is interesting to me because studying is something almost everyone has experience with, and poor planning can affect both results and wellbeing. I think this problem is very common, especially among students who have many courses at the same time, work part-time, or have other responsibilities outside school. A tool like this could make everyday life easier and reduce unnecessary stress.

The project tries to solve problems such as:
* difficulty managing time
* stress caused by many deadlines at once
* uncertainty about what task should be done first
* poor balance between school, free time, and rest
* last-minute studying

## How is it used?

The idea is that the user opens the app or website and enters information about their courses, assignments, deadlines, exam dates, and maybe also how many hours they are available to study during the week. After that, the AI system creates a suggested plan.

For example, if a student has an exam in one course next week and a written assignment due in another course in three days, the tool could recommend which tasks should be prioritized first. It could also divide larger tasks into smaller parts, such as:
* read chapter 1 on Monday
* make notes on Tuesday
* solve practice questions on Wednesday
* revise on Thursday

The user could then follow the plan, change it if needed, and give feedback on whether the suggestions were useful. Over time, the system could adapt to the student’s habits. For example, if the student usually studies better in the evening than in the morning, the planner could take that into account.

The main users would be students in high school, university, or other education programs. It could also be useful for adult learners who are studying while working. The design should be simple and clear, because students already have enough stress and do not need a complicated system.

## Data sources and AI methods

This kind of project would depend on different kinds of data. Some of the data could be entered directly by the user, such as:
* course names
* deadlines
* exam dates
* study goals
* available study hours
* personal preferences

The system could also use data from calendars or learning platforms if connected to them. For example, it could import deadlines automatically from a digital school platform or from the user’s calendar.

Possible AI methods for this project could include:
* recommendation systems, to suggest what the student should work on next
* machine learning, to learn from the student’s habits and improve future plans
* prediction models, to estimate how much time different tasks may require
* natural language processing, if the student writes tasks in their own words
* optimization methods, to build a schedule that fits into the student’s available time

The project does not need very advanced AI in the beginning. A simple version could already be useful if it can prioritize tasks in a smart way and create a weekly study plan.

## Challenges

This project would not solve every problem related to studying. A planning tool can help with structure, but it cannot fully solve lack of motivation, tiredness, mental health issues, or difficult personal situations. It also depends on the user entering correct and complete information. If deadlines are missing or if the student does not update the plan, the suggestions may be less useful.

Another challenge is that people study in very different ways. Some like to work in long sessions, while others prefer short sessions with breaks. Some people learn best by reading, while others prefer exercises or videos. This means the system should not assume that one study method works for everyone.

There are also privacy concerns. A tool like this could collect personal information about schedules, habits, and performance. That means the data would need to be handled carefully and stored securely. It is important that users understand what data is collected and how it is used.

## What next?

The first next step would be to create a simple prototype. In that version, a student could enter deadlines and available study time, and the system would generate a basic weekly plan. After that, the project could be improved by adding reminders, calendar integration, and smarter recommendations.

Later versions could include:
* progress tracking
* personalized study suggestions
* better predictions about how long tasks will take
* support for group work
* integration with school platforms
* features that help students reflect on their study habits

In the long term, this project could become a useful everyday tool for students. It could also be expanded to support teachers, who might want to understand when students are overloaded with deadlines.
